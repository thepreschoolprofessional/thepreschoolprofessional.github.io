One real weekday, start to finish — background steps run on their own; anything that submits, posts, or messages a parent waits for your approval.

4:30 AM
Readiness check

Every login and connection the day depends on is verified — if anything needs you, it's the first thing you see, before problems can happen.

5:00 AM
Phone dashboards refresh

Today-at-a-glance, mission control, finance, and social dashboards regenerate — check on everything from your phone, anywhere.

5:45 AM
Financial briefing

Yesterday's transactions pulled and categorized, cash position and payroll ratio posted — you know where both schools stand before sunrise.

6:00 AM
Director briefings, weather & email triage #1

Each director gets a school-specific action board; your inbox gets the first of three daily sweeps — summarized, drafted, flagged.

6:45 AM
ProCare morning sync

Check-in data syncs to the desktop system before doors open — so the day starts with clean records at both schools.

7:00 AM
Your action board + families hear from you

Approvals first, then the day in priority order. Parent messages go out in your voice: the mid-week tip, the Friday recap, tuition reminders on the right days.

8:05 AM
Staff culture moment

The morning teacher quote posts to each school — translated into Spanish and Hindi so every teacher starts the day included.

9:00 AM
Parent message check #1 + ratio check

Every ProCare parent message is read — absences routed, questions surfaced. First live staffing-ratio check against state rules.

10–11 AM
Hourly ratio checks + approvals digest

Staffing re-checked as children arrive, and the first "waiting on you" digest lists everything that needs your yes.

12:00 PM
Teachers get answers + payroll pull

The lesson-plan assistant answers teachers with the right plan and activities. Every other Wednesday, payroll hours are gathered and checked against revenue.

2:30 PM
After-nap ratio check

The tricky after-nap window — children signed in vs staff on the floor vs state ratios, with combine / float / cut-hours advice if anything is off.

3:00 PM
ProCare → KidKare attendance & meals

Check-ins pulled, meals matched to serving windows and recorded in KidKare for the food program claim, running records updated for month-end.

4:00 PM
School two + email triage #3 + ELC records

The second school's attendance and meals recorded, inboxes swept again, and SR attendance kept ready for the ELC portal — always audit-ready.

6:00 PM
Photos become posts + hours pace

Today's classroom photos gathered and captioned for social. Every staff member's hours projected — overtime spotted days before it happens.

8 PM – Midnight
Tomorrow gets prepped, today gets logged

Tomorrow's director briefings are staged, and the entire day — every task, every result — is written to a log so nothing is ever lost.

Weekends
It covers you when you’re closed

Every inbox gets a warm "we’re closed, back Monday" reply with your registration link, and the weekend family guide goes out — enrollment never sleeps.

That’s 30–40 background and approval-ready runs every weekday — before the weekly, monthly, and seasonal work stacked on top.

The Real Difference

Not magic — just less on your plate.

Running a school will always take a human. This carries the repetitive weight, works in real time, and makes the rest simple enough to hand off.

About an hour a day

Most of the daily grind runs on its own. What's left — the quick logins and approvals — takes roughly an hour, the same short routine every day.

Anyone can run it

It's a simple, repeatable routine, not a skill. Train any team member to run the daily steps in an afternoon — so it's never all on you.

Real-time ratios

See staff-to-child ratios against state rules as the day changes — know instantly when you can cut hours or need coverage.

Staff sign-in / sign-out

Clock-ins and clock-outs are tracked and cleaned up daily, feeding straight into payroll and coverage — no more chasing the paper book.

It fixes itself

When something looks off — a stuck punch, a missing day, a wrong number — the system catches it, suggests the fix, and flags it for your review — nothing changes without your approval.

Records that build up

Daily attendance and meals are logged after your daily approval, then roll up so month-end work like ELC is a quick review — not a from-scratch scramble.

It all connects

Attendance feeds the food program, staffing feeds payroll, payments feed your finances — one system, everything talking to everything.

Run it from your phone

Away from the school? Send a request from your phone and it happens in real time — post to parents, check a number, or kick off a task — you approve before anything public goes out.

Never a black box

Any time a task runs, you can open it and watch it work live — every step narrated, every run saved, so you can review anything, any time.

Security & Privacy

Your families' trust is the whole business.

Built by an owner who answers to licensing, subsidy programs, and — most importantly — parents. Security is how the system is designed, not a feature bolted on at the end.

You hold every key

You handle every login and multi-factor step yourself — the system waits until you confirm you're signed in before doing anything. It never sees or stores a password.

Children's data stays put

Child records never leave the software you already use. The system works directly inside your own attendance, food-program, and state-portal accounts — no separate copy is stored anywhere.

Approval gates on everything public

Nothing reaches parents, social media, or the public without your explicit yes — every message is drafted for your review and only sends once you approve the exact wording.

Official filings stay human

Daily records are saved after your one approval. The actual monthly claim to a food program or state coalition is always a separate, deliberate action a person takes — never automated.

It never moves money

The system doesn't process payments, payroll transfers, or purchases. Where it touches financial-adjacent data, like staffing hours, it's read-only — it never edits or moves anything.

A full audit trail

Every day's attendance, meal counts, and submissions are saved to dated files you control, so you can review exactly what happened. First names only, photo-by-photo approval, no-photo lists honored.

The Story Behind It

I built this because I needed it.

As a wife, mother of four, multiple preschool owner, and a USAF veteran, I bring a lot to early education — and I hold myself to a high standard. I have a Master's in Business and I'm currently pursuing my doctoral in Early Childhood Education, because I believe this work deserves real expertise and real systems. In the Air Force I learned that excellence isn't an accident; it's a system.

Alongside running my schools, I've published over 20 children's books — including the Your Feelings Matter with Mrs. Bear series — ten eBooks, and a children's album, Welcome to Success, that teaches phonics, music, and movement. But for years, the admin behind the schools ran me — attendance, the food program, ELC, payroll, parent updates — all before I could focus on the children.

So I built a system that carries it for me. Today my schools run with excellence, largely on their own, and I get to do what I set out to do — prepare children for school while instilling confidence and self-belief. This is that system, packaged so any owner can have what I have.

— Mrs. Bear
