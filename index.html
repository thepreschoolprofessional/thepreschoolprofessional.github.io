Readiness check

5:00 AM

Phone dashboards refresh

5:45 AM

Financial briefing

Yesterday's transactions pulled and categorized, cash position and payroll ratio posted — you know exactly where both schools stand before sunrise.

6:00 AM

Director briefings, weather & email triage #1

Each director gets a school-specific action board — deadlines, follow-ups, today's forecast, and a GO / WATCH call on field-trip days. Your inbox gets its first of three daily sweeps: summarized, replies drafted, school items flagged.

6:10 AM

Office inboxes triaged — both schools

Each school's office email is read for you — licensing notices, food program letters, subsidy emails — actionable items flagged, obvious replies drafted, briefing items queued.

6:45 AM

ProCare morning sync

Check-in data syncs to the desktop system before doors open — so the day starts with clean records at both schools.

7:00 AM

Your action board + families hear from you

Your personal morning board lands — approvals first, then the day in priority order. Parent messages go out in your voice: the mid-week "Teddy Bear Talk" tip, the Friday recap, tuition reminders on the right days.

8:05 AM

Staff culture moment

The morning teacher quote posts to each school — translated into Spanish and Hindi so every teacher starts the day included and inspired. Fridays add the bilingual teacher heads-up for next week.

9:00 AM

Parent message check #1 + ratio check

Every ProCare parent message at both schools is read — absences routed straight to each school's to-do list, questions surfaced for a quick reply. First live staffing-ratio check against state rules.

9:30 AM

Staff punches cleaned up

Yesterday's time clocks finalized — overnight stuck punches, missing sign-ins, and no-shows flagged while directors can still fix them.

10 – 11 AM

Hourly ratio checks + approvals digest

Staffing re-checked as children arrive, and the first "waiting on you" digest lists everything that needs your yes — nothing sits forgotten.

12:00 PM

Teachers get answers + payroll pull

The lesson-plan assistant answers teachers' questions with the right plan and activity ideas. Every other Wednesday, payroll hours are gathered and checked against revenue for entry.

1:00 PM

Email triage #2 — round two

Your inbox and both office inboxes swept again — anything that landed since morning is summarized, drafted, or flagged before it can pile up.

2:30 PM

After-nap ratio check

The tricky after-nap window — children signed in vs staff on the floor vs state ratios, with combine / float / cut-hours advice if anything is off.

3:00 PM

ProCare → KidKare attendance & meals

The daily attendance chain begins: check-ins pulled from ProCare, meals matched to serving windows and recorded in KidKare for the food program claim, running records updated for month-end. Afternoon punch check fixes time clocks before close.

4:00 PM

School two + email triage #3 + ELC records

The second school's attendance and meals are recorded, your inboxes get their third sweep, directors are reminded to check the mail, and SR attendance is kept ready for the ELC portal — subsidy records always audit-ready.

6:00 PM

Photos become posts + hours pace

Today's classroom photos are gathered and captioned for social media. Every staff member's hours are finalized and projected against schedules — overtime spotted days before it happens. Thursdays also capture the weekly billing reports.

7:00 PM

Parent message check #2

The evening sweep of ProCare messages — anything parents sent after pickup is routed and waiting for the right person by morning.

8:00 PM — MIDNIGHT

Tomorrow gets prepped, today gets logged

Tomorrow's director briefings are researched and staged, and the entire day — every task, every result — is written to a log so nothing is ever lost. Then it starts again.

WEEKENDS

It covers you when you're closed

Every inbox gets a warm "we're closed, back Monday" reply with your registration link, and the weekend family guide of free local events goes out — enrollment never sleeps, even when you do.

That's 30–40 automated runs every weekday — before counting the weekly, monthly, and seasonal work stacked on top.

The Real Difference

Not magic — just less on your plate.

Be honest: running a school will always take a human. What this does is carry the repetitive weight, work in real time, and make the rest simple enough to hand off. Here's what that actually looks like.

About an hour a day

Most of the daily grind runs on its own. What's left — the quick logins and approvals — takes roughly an hour, and it's the same short routine every day.

Anyone can run it

It's a simple, repeatable routine, not a skill. You can train any team member to run the daily steps in an afternoon — so it's never all on you.

Real-time ratios

See your staff-to-child ratios against state rules as the day changes — know instantly when you can cut hours or need coverage.

Staff sign-in / sign-out

Clock-ins and clock-outs are tracked and cleaned up daily, feeding straight into payroll and coverage — no more chasing the paper book.

It fixes itself

When something looks off — a stuck punch, a missing day, a wrong number — the system catches it, corrects what it can, and flags the rest instead of failing silently.

Records that build up

Daily attendance and meals are logged automatically, then roll up so month-end work like ELC is a quick review — not a from-scratch scramble.

It all connects

Attendance feeds the food program, staffing feeds payroll, payments feed your finances — one system, everything talking to everything.

Run it from your phone

Away from the school? Send a request from your phone and it happens in real time — post to parents, check a number, kick off a task — even when you're nowhere near a computer.

Honest & in your control

Some steps use secure sites that need a real login — so you (or your director) sign in, and the system does the rest. You always hold the keys.

👀 Never a black box.

This is not a blind operation. Any time a task is running, you can open it and watch it work — live. Every step is narrated in real time: what it's reading, what it's recording, what it decided and why. Most days you won't need to look — that's the beauty of it. But the window is always open, and the full step-by-step record of every single run is saved, so you can review anything, any time.

Security & Privacy

Your families' trust is the whole business.

This was built by an owner who answers to licensing, subsidy programs, and — most importantly — parents. Security isn't a feature bolted on at the end; it's how the system is designed.

🔑 You hold every key

Your passwords are never stored, shared, or handed to the system. Secure portals — banking, subsidy, licensing — always get a real human sign-in from you or your director.

🛡️ Children's data stays put

Child records are never copied into outside services. The system works inside the platforms you already use and trust — your childcare software, food program, and subsidy portals.

✋ Approval gates on everything public

Nothing reaches parents, social media, or the public without your explicit yes — every photo, every post, every message is approved by a human first.

🧾 Official filings stay human

Government claims and subsidy submissions are prepared and organized for you — but a person always reviews and presses submit. The system records; you certify.

💵 It never moves money

The system watches your finances and preps payroll — but it never places orders on its own, never transfers funds, never touches your books. Your accountant's lane stays your accountant's lane.

📓 A full audit trail

Every action is logged nightly — what ran, when, and what it touched. Privacy-first publishing too: first names only, photo-by-photo approval, and no-photo lists honored.
